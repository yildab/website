I am the Chairperson of the [Innovation, Diversity, Exploration & Advancement in STEM (IDEAS) Initiative](https://www.queensu.ca/physics/ideas), a volunteer organization which aims to encourage the advancement of under-represented groups in physics. 

**IDEAS Summer Camp**

The main program organized by IDEAS is the IDEAS Camp, a free, week-long physics day camp. Campers aged 11 - 14 participated in laboratory experiments, workshops, and a group design project. I was the Camp Director for the 2023 and 2024 iterations of the Camp!

![A student design project on the topic of dark matter.](/IDEAS1.jpeg)

<!--more-->

In the summer of 2025, in lieu of the IDEAS Camp, we facilitated a partnership with the Queen's MINI-Q Arts and Science Summer Camp, offering a series of astroparticle-themed summer camp activities for their campers (aged 5-11).

**Let's Talk Astrophysics Symposium 2021**

The IDEAS Initiative and Let's Talk Science held a 2-day virtual symposium aimed at high school students on the topic of astrophysics. [Here](https://www.queensu.ca/physics/lets-talk-astrophysics-symposium) is a summary of the event!

I was the Materials Assistant, and part of the development team for the coding workshop. In this workshop, students used public observation data to construct galaxy rotation curves and search for signs of dark matter, while learning to code in Python. [Here](https://github.com/LTSQueens/Let-s-Talk-Astrophysics?tab=readme-ov-file) is the GitHub repository which hosts the JupyterNotebook for the activity.
